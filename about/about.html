What's Going On?


In fall of 2023, the CSU Board of Trustees voted to implement a yearly tuition increase of 6% over the course of five years. This is the first year it comes into effect. According to the SFSU financial aid website, the average tuition cost is $8,256 for two semesters for the 2025-26 school year. This means that in five years the average cost will be 10,732 for just tuition. On top of all this, around 300 lectures are being laid off which results in us as students paying more for less.

How does this affect you, the student?

Some important statistics


The obvious answer is you will be paying more money. If you are an out of state student, you’re paying around $40K (depending on if any other fees increase as well) per year and 10K if you’re a commuter student. Beyond that, you may need to pay even more if you’re not able to graduate on time because of professor layoffs and class cuts. All of SF State’s financial burdens fall on us, all while the administers’ salaries increase by twice the rate than that of the teachers.

Last semester, we lost 313 sections from our previous 2,991 total class sections. Now, we are expecting to lose even more after having another round of budget cuts. Since 2020, class sections have declined by 29% over five years. Conversely, tuition since 2020 has gone from 7,006 to 8,256, demonstrating that we are getting less education for more money. Now, with the 6% increase annually and even more cuts to education locally and federally, we are as students being harmed long term.

How Budget cuts are affecting SFSU


Faculty and Lectures

  • Approximately 325 lecturers, representing about 30% of SFSU's lecturer workforce, are being laid off.
  • The English Department is also impacted, with 19 lecturers not returning for the Spring 2025 semester.
  • Lecturers constitute 60% of the university's faculty, and many have expressed concerns over job security and the impact on student education.
  • Plans include eliminating 23 full-time, tenure-track faculty positions. ​California Faculty Association

    • Staff and Administrative Positions

    Layoffs are affecting various staff classifications, including:

  • Groundsworkers, Custodians, Laborers
  • Administrative Support Assistants and Coordinators
  • Instructional Support Assistants I–III
  • Research Technicians
  • Facilities Maintenance Mechanics, Painters, and Building Service Engineers

    • Layoff determinations are based on classifications and seniority, with temporary and probationary employees being the first affected.



    Academic and Research Programs

    Estuary and Ocean Science Center:

  • The only marine lab on San Francisco Bay is closing due to a $25 million budget shortfall.
  • Despite a recent $5.8 million grant from NOAA, the center faces a $600,000 funding gap.
  • The closure impacts research on water quality, climate change, and marine restoration projects.

    • Athletics

    Sports Teams:

  • Baseball
  • Men's Soccer
  • Woman's Indoor Track and Field
  • These cuts, effective July 1, 2025, aim to save up to $1 million annually and affect 44 student-athletes.
  • Athletes will retain their scholarships despite the program eliminations.

    • Course Offering and Student Impact

    Course Reduction:

  • Spring 2025 course offerings have decreased by 10.8% compared to Spring 2024, dropping from 2,991 to 2,678 sections.
  • This reduction is part of a broader trend, with a 29% decrease in course offerings since Spring 2020.

    • Student Concerns:

  • Students report difficulties enrolling in required courses, which can potentially delay graduation.
  • Larger class sizes and fewer course options are additional concerns raised by the student body.

    • Financial Context

    Enrollment Decline:

    SFSU's enrollment has declined from nearly 29,000 students in 2019 to just over 22,300 in Fall 2024.


    Budget Deficit:

    The university faces a $25 million budget deficit, prompting widespread cuts across departments and programs.


    System-Wide Challenges:

    The CSU system anticipates a projected $375 million budget cut for the 2025-26 fiscal year, which will affect multiple campuses, including SFSU.
